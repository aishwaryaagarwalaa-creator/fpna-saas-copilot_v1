# SaaS Finance Glossary

## Subscription Revenue
Recurring revenue earned from SaaS subscription contracts.
Typically recognized ratably over the contract period.

## Professional Services Revenue
Revenue earned from implementation, consulting, or support services.
Generally non-recurring and lower margin than subscription revenue.

## Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
Direct costs required to deliver the SaaS product, including:
- Hosting and infrastructure
- Customer support
- Third-party service costs

## Gross Margin
Gross margin represents revenue minus COGS, expressed as a percentage of revenue.
In SaaS businesses, gross margin is a key indicator of scalability and unit economics.

## Operating Expenses (Opex)
Expenses incurred to operate the business, excluding COGS.
Includes Sales & Marketing, Research & Development, and General & Administrative costs.

## Operating Margin
Operating margin represents operating income as a percentage of revenue.
It reflects the balance between growth investment and cost discipline.

## Deferred Revenue
Cash received from customers for services that have not yet been delivered.
Deferred revenue represents future revenue to be recognized.

## Accounts Receivable
Amounts billed to customers but not yet collected in cash.

## Cash and Cash Equivalents
Highly liquid funds available to support ongoing operations and growth.

## Current Ratio
A liquidity metric calculated as current assets divided by current liabilities.
A ratio above 1.0 indicates sufficient short-term liquidity.
